What You Should Have Learned

 

When you have finished reading the essay “From Hoover to Johnson: A Supply-Side Take” and read the transcript of, or listened to, a recording of John F. Kennedy’s “Address to the Economic Club of New York,” you should understand the following concepts:

 

• Anti-supply-side policymaking of Presidents Hoover and Roosevelt were key     factors in bringing about the end of the "Roaring Twenties" and ushering in the            Great Depression.

 

• The policies of these two Presidents helped to deepen the Great Depression by:

            Trade protectionism

            Huge tax increases

            Large increases in government spending, and

            A vast expansion in government regulation.

 

• World War II replaced the Great Depression with a new set of economic troubles:

            A decline in business investment and

            A decline in consumer spending.

 

• Sky-high, burdensome taxes continued to restrain the American economy after World War II into the early 1960s.

 

• President John F. Kennedy played a leading role in bringing supply-side concepts back into the tax policy debate:

 

            JFK countered the importance of increased government spending

 

            JFK emphasized tax cuts that enhance “incentives for personal effort,        investment, and risk-taking."

 

• JFK advocated two tax cuts that, as predicted by supply-side economics:

            Increased the rewards of working,

            Increased the rewards for saving

            Increased the rewards for investing,

 

• President Lyndon B. Johnson later enacted a temporary income tax increase that negatively affected the economy, including the impact on investment.
